01 / What is Swarm Intelligence?

At its core, swarm intelligence is about emergence: achieving complex, "intelligent" group behavior using very simple underlying rules.

Key Properties of a Swarm:

  • Greater than the sum of its parts: The swarm appears smarter than any individual member.
  • Lack of awareness: An individual (like a single fish or ant) is usually unaware of the "big picture" or the complex structure they are building.
  • Robustness: Because the behavior is distributed, the system doesn't fail if one individual does. If one fish is eaten, the school keeps rolling.
  • Stigmergy: This is the secret sauce. It's the "indirect sharing of information through changes in the environment—like ants leaving pheromone trails or humans editing Wikipedia.

02 / Two Powerhouse Algorithms

There are two primary algorithms we use to turn these biological observations into math: Ant Colony Optimisation (ACO) and Particle Swarm Optimisation (PSO).

1. Ant Colony Optimisation (ACO)

Designed by Marco Dorigo in 1992, ACO is the go-to for combinatorial optimisation—finding the best order for things (like the Traveling Salesman Problem).
How it works: It mimics how ants forage for food.
The Process: Ants explore paths randomly and leave "pheromones" behind.
The Shortcut: Shorter paths allow ants to travel back and forth faster, laying down more pheromones.
Result: Over time, the pheromones on the longer paths evaporate, and the swarm converges on the most efficient route.

2. Particle Swarm Optimisation (PSO)

Invented in 1995, PSO is inspired by the "Boids" model of bird flocking. It treats the search for a solution like a swarm of particles flying through a "fitness landscape".
The Rules of the Flight:
1. Separation: Don't crowd your neighbors.
2. Alignment: Move in the same direction of the group.
3. Cohesion: Move toward the center of the group.
The Math of Sucess: Each particle remembers its own Personal Best (Cognitive Component) and knows the Global Best of the swarm (Social component). The update equation: v[i] = w * v[i] + c1 * r1 * (pBest[i] - x[i]) + c2 * r2 * (gBest - x[i])
Where:
- v[i] is the velocity of particle i
- w is the inertia weight
- c1 and c2 are the cognitive and social coefficients
- r1 and r2 are random numbers between 0 and 1
- pBest[i] is the best position particle i has found
- gBest is the best position found by the entire swarm
Result: Particles "fly" through the solution space, influenced by their own experience and the success of their neighbors, eventually converging on an optimal or near-optimal solution.
